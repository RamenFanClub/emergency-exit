01 · The name

Kin + light

Kin is who shows up — family by blood or by choice, the people on your list. Light is the promise — something kept burning so they're never left in the dark.

A kinlight is the lamp a keeper tends for the people out on the water. While you check in, the light stays on. If one day you can't, the light does its job: it guides your people safely to everything you prepared for them.

Always written as one word. In running text: Kinlight. In the wordmark only: lowercase kinlight, with "light" in lamplight amber.

02 · Brand world

The lighthouse, the harbour, the anchor

Three images, three jobs. They live in the logo, the copy, and the moments of reassurance — never as the name, never as decoration for its own sake.

The lighthouse — for you

You are the keeper. Checking in is tending the light. This frames the product's only recurring chore as an act of care, not a deadline.

The safe harbour — for your things

The vault is the harbour: assets, wishes, and letters moored somewhere calm and protected until they're needed.

The anchor — for your people

What recipients get is steadiness in the worst week of their lives: one email, everything in order, nothing to chase. The anchor language belongs on the recipient side of the product.

03 · Voice

Calm, plain, certain

Kinlight speaks like a capable friend who has done this before: short sentences, plain verbs, no drama. It never makes death feel closer, and it never makes the product feel cleverer than the person using it.

Always

  • "Your light is on." — state, plainly
  • "Checked in ✓ — see you next time."
  • "Everything is in order."
  • "If you ever stop checking in…" — conditional, gentle
  • Active voice, sentence case, one job per sentence

Never

  • "When you die…" / "after your death" — in-app
  • "Don't worry!" — reassurance that names the worry
  • Exclamation marks in any timer or notification
  • Nautical puns ("anchors aweigh", "smooth sailing")
  • Promising features that don't exist yet

That last "never" is your UX review's false-confidence theme, made law: the brand voice may only describe protection that is actually built.

04 · Colour

Daylight palette, one night moment

The palette is Aeterna Solid, unchanged — renamed so every colour knows its job in the lighthouse story. The app lives in daylight. The dark night water tone is reserved for brand moments only (this guide's cover, the email avatar, the app icon) — it's what the light shines against, so it must stay rare.

05 · Typography

Manrope speaks, Public Sans explains

Display · Manrope 800, tight tracking
Everything is in order.
Body · Public Sans 400/600

Your next check-in is due in 30 days. A quick tap keeps your light on and your timer reset — that's all there is to it.

Both faces are already shipped in the app; the rename costs nothing here. Display lines are short, declarative, and end with a full stop — the full stop is part of the calm.

07 · The recipient moment

Where the brand earns its keep

A recipient meets Kinlight twice: once when they're nominated (F61 — calm, consent, recognition) and once when it matters most (F64 — never anonymous about who arranged it). The sender identity is the same in both: Kinlight <hello@kinlight.app>, with SPF/DKIM configured in Resend before the first real send.

Delivery email (F64)

Kinlight <hello@kinlight.app>
To: Jane Doe
Subject: Sarah arranged for this to reach you

Hello Jane,

Sarah Chen used Kinlight to prepare something for you. She asked that it be delivered if she ever stopped checking in — and that time has come. We're so sorry.

Attached is everything she put in order for you: her wishes, important details about her assets and documents, and a personal letter she wrote for you.

📎  Sarah-Chen-for-Jane-Doe.pdf

There's nothing you need to do right away. This package was prepared in advance by Sarah herself, and it will be here whenever you're ready.

— Kinlight, a light left on for the people you love

Three rules visible above, now standing policy: the vault holder is always named (subject line and first sentence), the email never instructs urgency, and condolence is one sentence, plain — "We're so sorry," nothing more ornate.

Nomination email (F61) — opening lines

Subject: Sarah added you as a trusted contact on Kinlight

"Hello Jane — Sarah Chen has named you as someone she trusts. Kinlight is a service that keeps her important wishes and information safe, and delivers them to her chosen people if she's ever unable to. You don't need to do anything now. This email is just so you recognise us if we ever need to reach you on Sarah's behalf."

08 · The mark

The lantern

The logo is a harbour lantern — the vessel built to keep a flame burning through wind and weather. A sage frame holds an amber glow; the white inner highlight is the flame itself. No lighthouse tower, because the building was never the point — the kept light is. It says exactly what the product does: it protects the light so it doesn't go out.

Night tile for the app icon and email avatar; daylight tile for in-app and documents.

In the live app, the flame may carry the same gentle 4.5s pulse as the check-in card — the light breathing, never blinking. At favicon size (≤16px) the handle drops and the glowing body carries the mark. Respect prefers-reduced-motion.
